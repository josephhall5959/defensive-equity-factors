# Do Defensive Equity Factors Deliver? Conditional Factor Premia over the U.S. Business Cycle, 1963–2026

A fully reproducible empirical asset-pricing paper, produced end-to-end as a
generative-AI research workflow for the **AFA 2027 Special Session on papers
written with generative-AI workflows**.

## Research question

A large practitioner literature markets "quality," profitability, and
low-investment strategies as *defensive*. This paper tests that claim by
measuring the realized premia of the five Fama–French factors plus momentum
conditional on the NBER business-cycle state. The headline finding: the market
and momentum are strongly **procyclical** (momentum suffers a −34% crash in
April 2009), the profitability (RMW) and investment (CMA) factors are genuinely
**defensive** (premia and Sharpe ratios *rise* in recessions), and size and
value are acyclical. The pattern survives a real-time, tradeable bear-market
signal and is visible in event time around peaks and troughs.

## Reproduce everything

Requirements: `R` (with `sandwich`, `lmtest`), a LaTeX toolchain
(`pdflatex`, `bibtex`), and `make`.

```bash
make all        # run the R pipeline AND compile the PDF
# or step by step:
make pipeline   # Rscript run_all.R  -> tables + figures
make paper      # compile paper/paper.pdf
```

`make distclean && make all` rebuilds from nothing, including re-downloading the
raw data. The R package dependencies install to a personal library; see
`run_all.R`.

## Design notes

- **Reproducibility first.** Every number in the manuscript is generated by the
  R pipeline and injected via `output/tables/numbers.tex` macros — nothing is
  hand-transcribed, so the prose cannot drift from the data.
- **No look-ahead.** Section 5 replaces the (un-investable) NBER dating with a
  trailing-12-month market-return signal known in real time, and recovers the
  same cross-sectional pattern.
- **Robust inference.** All t-statistics use Newey–West HAC standard errors with
  automatic lag selection.
